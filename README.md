# Labor Substitution and AI Automation Risk Analysis

This repository contains the code, dataset, and comprehensive report for my Data Science project analyzing the economic threat of AI labor substitution across various industries.

## Project Overview
With the rapid advancement of Artificial Intelligence, evaluating which jobs are at risk of automation is critical. This project applies Data Science techniques to analyze a curated economic dataset, exploring the financial tipping point where running an AI agent becomes cheaper than human labor.

### Key Objectives
* Perform rigorous data cleaning and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on global labor cost data.
* Visualize the impact of regulatory barriers and hardware costs on automation speed.
* Build a predictive Simple Linear Regression model to forecast the future cost of AI agents based on projected 2026 inference compute costs.

## Technologies Used
* **Python**
* **Pandas & NumPy** (Data Manipulation)
* **Matplotlib & Seaborn** (Data Visualization)
* **Scikit-Learn** (Linear Regression Modeling)

## Key Findings
1.  **Regulatory Moats Matter:** Laws and compliance act as massive shields against immediate automation.
2.  **The Compute Factor:** Linear Regression proved that the dropping cost of cloud compute is the primary driver of digital labor viability.
3.  **Augmentation vs. Substitution:** Many industries won't lose jobs; they will evolve into Human-AI symbiotic roles.
